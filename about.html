Most coaches talk about leadership.
I've lived it. And you will too.

I see what's really driving you and your team. And what's getting in the way.

For 15 years I was the Artistic Director at The Marriott Theatre, one of Chicago's premier live performance venues. I led creative teams through high-stakes, high-pressure productions where everything depended on trust, timing, and people being honest with each other in real time.

That experience gave me something most business consultants don't have: the ability to read a room at two levels. I see the organizational structure — the strategy, the roles, the decision-making patterns, where accountability breaks down. But I also see the internal structure of each leader — the motivations, the fears, the stories they're telling themselves that drive every decision they make.

When a CFO avoids conflict, the budget process breaks. When a CEO holds too tight, nobody else takes ownership. When a COO is afraid of looking incompetent, problems get hidden instead of solved. Most consultants see the symptom. I see the engine underneath it.

That's what I bring to every engagement. Whether I'm working with a full executive team or coaching a single leader one-on-one, the work starts with seeing the whole picture — the business and the people running it.

How I Work

Here's what I won't do.

I won't give you a personality test and call it coaching. I won't nod along and tell you you're doing great when you're not. I won't run a one-day offsite and disappear. And I won't tiptoe around the thing everyone in the room already knows but nobody will say.

If your team has a trust problem, I'll name it. If you're the bottleneck, I'll name that too. If a leader on your team is checked out or playing politics, we're going to talk about it — directly and with care.

I work with leaders who want the truth, not the comfortable version of it. And I stay long enough to make sure something actually changes. Not a month. Months. Until the new habits stick and the team operates differently without me in the room.
