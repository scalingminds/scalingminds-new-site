You have a problem.
Call Andy.

I've spent 20 years in rooms where the stakes are high and the truth matters.

For 17 years I was the Artistic Director at The Marriott Theatre, one of Chicago's premier live performance venues. Every night, I watched what happened when talented people trusted each other enough to do extraordinary work. And what happened when they didn't.

That experience taught me something most business consultants never learn firsthand: the difference between a group of talented individuals and a real team isn't strategy or skill. It's trust, honesty, and shared accountability.

Today I work with CEOs, leadership teams, and senior leaders at privately held businesses and nonprofits. I created The Six Shifts™ because I kept seeing the same patterns: smart people, good intentions, and a team that couldn't get out of its own way.

How I Work

I'm not the coach who tells you what you want to hear.

I name what I see.

Most teams know something is off. They just can't name it, or won't. I will. Directly, respectfully, and in a way that moves the conversation forward instead of shutting it down.

I push and I care.

Compassion without accountability is just cheerleading. Accountability without compassion is just pressure. I bring both. My clients will tell you I'm the person who believed in them more than they believed in themselves and held them to a higher standard at the same time.

I stay until it sticks.

I don't do one-off workshops and walk away. I work with teams and leaders over months, building the habits and rhythms that make change last. Real transformation doesn't happen in a single session. It happens in the follow-through.
