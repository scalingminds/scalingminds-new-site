Meet Andy

I ran a theater for 15 years before I ever coached an executive.

That's not a quirky backstory, it's the reason the work actually works. As Artistic Director at The Marriott Theatre for 15 years, I wasn't performing. I was responsible for the people who were. You learn to read a room the way most people read a spreadsheet. You learn that talented people don't automatically become a great ensemble, that trust isn't a soft skill but the infrastructure everything else runs on, and that the conversation nobody wants to have is almost always the one that changes everything. Most executive coaches come from business. I came from a world where you couldn't hide behind a slide deck. It's why a CEO like Scurry Miller at Children's Home trusted me with her entire C-Suite, and why their team operates differently now.

Today I work with leadership teams at privately held companies and mission-driven nonprofits, installing a system that changes how the team actually works together. I also coach individual CEOs and senior leaders through the moments where who they need to become matters more than what they need to do.

What We Do

How we work with leaders and their teams.

Executive Team Performance

Most leadership teams are a collection of strong individuals who happen to report to the same person, and that's the whole problem. I install the Six Shifts Leadership Operating System so the team that got you here can actually get you there. That means a diagnostic that shows you exactly where the team is stuck, monthly sessions that build new habits, 1:1 coaching for key leaders, and an embedded presence for 6 to 12 months. I stay until it sticks.

Senior Leader Coaching

For CEOs and senior leaders who need a thinking partner for the moments that matter most: scaling, transition, succession, or the realization that what got you here won't get you there. I surface the patterns you can't see from inside them. I challenge directly and support fully. And I help you close the gap between the leader you intend to be and the one who shows up under pressure. This isn't therapy, and it isn't cheerleading. It's a mirror and a system for becoming the leader your next chapter requires.
